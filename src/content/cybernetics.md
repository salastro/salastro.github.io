Interdisciplinary study of communication and control in living organisms and machines. Cybernetics unifies feedback mechanisms, information flow, and goal-directed behavior across biological, mechanical, and organizational systems.

## Foundational Principles

Cybernetics rests on three pillars:
1. **Circular causal systems**: Feedback loops create circular dependencies
2. **Self-regulation**: Systems maintain stability through feedback
3. **Information**: Communication and signals regulate behavior

## Key Concepts

### Negative Feedback
Deviation from setpoint triggers corrective action. Enables stability and goal-seeking behavior. Example: thermostat maintaining temperature.

### Positive Feedback
Deviation is amplified, driving change. Can lead to instability but enables growth and oscillations. Example: population dynamics, avalanches.

### Information and Control
Ashby's Law of Requisite Variety: controlling a system requires matching complexity of controller to complexity of system.

## Historical Development

- **Wiener (1948)**: First comprehensive formulation
- **von Bertalanffy**: General systems theory
- **Second-order cybernetics**: Observer becomes part of system
- **Modern applications**: AI, robotics, organizational design

## Distinctions

- **Engineering control**: Precise setpoints, linear systems
- **Biological cybernetics**: Goal-seeking, adaptation, learning
- **Social cybernetics**: Organizations, networks, emergence

## Contemporary Applications

- Artificial intelligence and learning systems
- Autonomous vehicles
- Smart homes and IoT
- Organizational management
- Biological homeostasis
